Who Is She ?

Mary Wilkes is an 86 year old woman Wilkes was born in Chicago, Illinois and graduated from Wellesley College in 1959 where she majored in philosophy and theology. Wilkes planned to become a lawyer, but was discouraged by friends and mentors A geography teacher in the eighth grade had told Wilkes, "Mary Allen, when you grow up, you ought to be a computer programmer."She worked in the field as one of the first programmers for a number of years before pursuing law and becoming an attorney in 1975.

Career

  • 1950s - Teenage Years - Desire: Wilkes aspired to become a lawyer, unaware of computers.
  • 1959 - Graduation from Wellesley College - Wilkes completed her education at Wellesley College.
  • 1959 - Career Shift to Computer Programming
    - Graduation Day: Wilkes abandoned legal ambitions.
    - MIT Job: Hired as a computer programmer at MIT due to logical thinking skills and emerging opportunities in the field.
    - Joined LINC development team.
  • 1960s - Early Career as a Computer Programmer
    - Role: Wilkes wrote software for individual computer users.
    - Teaching: Instructed LINC users and co-authored its training manual.
    - Innovation: Became the first person to own a personal computer at home.
  • 1972 - Harvard Law School Acceptance
    - Transition: After eleven years in computer programming, Wilkes pursued law studies at Harvard.
  • Post-1972 - Career as a Lawyer
    - Profession: Wilkes embarked on a forty-year career as a lawyer after graduating from Harvard Law School.

Greatest invention

Mary Wilkes was the brains behind the LINC computer, aka the world's first “personal computer”.
Her use of the LINC computer at home in 1965 also makes her the first-ever person to use a home computer.
